## Measuring Cognitive Capacity

To measure your brain's capacity for focus and controlling thoughts, you need to evaluate core executive functions like inhibitory control, working memory, and task-switching. You can check these parameters using several clinical tools and cognitive tests:

### 1. Inhibitory Control (Thought Control)
The ability to stop an automatic impulse and control what you pay attention to.
- **How to measure:** The **Stroop Color and Word Test** is the gold standard. You are shown color words printed in different ink colors (e.g., the word "RED" in blue ink) and must name the ink color, inhibiting your automatic urge to read the word.

### 2. Working Memory
Your brain's "mental desk" that holds and manipulates information.
- **How to measure:** **Digit Span tests** (repeating a sequence of numbers backward) or performing consecutive subtractions (e.g., subtracting 7 from 100) measure how much information you can track while filtering distractions.

### 3. Cognitive Flexibility
The ability to switch between tasks and adapt your thinking.
- **How to measure:** **Trail Making Tests** (alternating connecting letters and numbers like 1-A-2-B) or the Wisconsin Card Sorting Test.

### Modern Tracking & Assessments
- **Wearable EEG Technology:** For real-time tracking, neurofeedback headbands (like Muse) monitor brainwaves to indicate how much time your brain spends in the focused Beta wave zone versus distracted zones.
- **Clinical Self-Assessments:** You can use standardized self-reporting tools like the Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale (ASRS-v1.1) or the Brown Attention-Deficit Disorder Scales to gauge how these functions operate in your daily life.
